Who I Am

I hold a Licence in Software Engineering from Université Constantine 2 (Algeria) and am currently completing my Bachelor's in Computer Science at Université Clermont Auvergne (France). Before engineering, I spent two years studying Fundamental Physics at USTHB — which shaped the way I approach problems: analytically, methodically, from first principles.

My work ranges from training deep learning models to designing full-stack web platforms with role-based access control, QR code systems, and real-time messaging. I'm equally comfortable in the backend designing REST APIs and JPA schemas, and in the frontend wiring up React components and async data flows. I've also gone low-level — building a compiler for a complex-number language and writing an x86 Assembly registration system from scratch.

Outside of code: I play chess, draw, ice skate, swim, and am picking up Russian and Spanish. I believe curiosity in all its forms makes a better developer.

Complex Number Compiler
compiler design · language implementation
A full compiler for a custom language supporting complex number arithmetic in algebraic, polar, and exponential forms. Targets the MVaP virtual machine with lazy boolean evaluation.
  • Three complex number representations: algebraic (a+ib), polar (r:θ), exponential (re^iθ)
  • Full arithmetic: addition, subtraction, multiplication, division, power, negation
  • Boolean expressions: ET, OU, NON, equality, ordering by modulus — with lazy evaluation
  • Variables, assignment, conditionals (lorsque/autrement), loops (repeter/jusque)
  • break and continue support inside loops
  • MVaP bytecode generation with PUSHF for complex float pairs
  • Operator precedence fully enforced in the grammar
